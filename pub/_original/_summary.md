## 1873-08

# August 1873: Paris and Return to Nice

## Overview

August 1873 marks a significant period in Marie Bashkirtseff's diary, documenting her visit to Paris and subsequent return to Nice. The month begins with Marie in Paris, where she engages in social activities, shopping, and cultural pursuits while harboring hopes of encountering the Duke of Hamilton. The latter half of the month chronicles her journey back to Nice and her reintegration into family life there, where her infatuation with the Duke becomes a topic of family discussion and teasing.

## Main Topics and Themes

### Paris Visit
- **Social Activities**: Marie attends various social gatherings, visits the Bois de Boulogne, and dines at fashionable establishments
- **Shopping and Fashion**: Extensive descriptions of visits to Worth and other fashion houses, ordering dresses and accessories
- **Cultural Pursuits**: Visits to the Louvre and other cultural sites, reflections on art and beauty
- **Unrequited Love**: Continued obsession with the Duke of Hamilton, disappointment at not seeing him in Paris

### Journey to Nice
- **Travel Observations**: Detailed account of the train journey from Paris to Nice, with stops in Mâcon, Marseille, and Toulon
- **Self-Reflection**: Contemplative moments during travel, considering her future and ambitions
- **Reluctant Return**: Expressions of disdain for Nice ("Paris que j'adore et Nice que j'abhorre")

### Family Dynamics in Nice
- **Family Teasing**: Marie's infatuation with the Duke becomes public knowledge within her family, leading to teasing
- **Mother's Opposition**: Her mother's skepticism about the Duke and preference for a Russian match
- **Social Status Concerns**: Discussions about the Duke's wealth and Marie's marriage prospects

## Key Events

1. **August 11-20**: Marie's time in Paris, filled with shopping, social visits, and cultural activities
2. **August 21-28**: Marie's journey from Paris back to Nice, with various stops along the way
3. **August 25-26**: Marie's emotional reaction to seeing Gioia's portrait at the Walery photography studio
4. **August 29**: News of Abramovitch's death, which deeply affects Marie
5. **August 30-31**: Family discussions about Marie's marriage prospects, with focus on the Duke of Hamilton

## People Mentioned

- **Duke of Hamilton**: The object of Marie's romantic obsession, discussed extensively in family conversations
- **Gioia**: A beautiful woman in Nice who Marie perceives as a rival, whose portrait Marie sees in Paris
- **Abramovitch**: A friend or acquaintance whose sudden death is reported on August 29
- **Princess Galitzine**: A family friend who encourages Marie's fantasies about the Duke, calling her "duchesse"
- **Walitsky**: A family acquaintance who teases Marie with stories about the Duke's eccentric behavior
- **Worth**: The famous fashion designer who Marie visits to order dresses
- **Various family members**: Mother, aunt, Dina (likely a cousin), and others who participate in discussions about Marie's future

## Social and Cultural Context

- **Fashion and Appearance**: Marie's detailed descriptions of clothing, accessories, and beauty routines reflect the importance of appearance in upper-class society
- **Marriage Prospects**: Discussions about potential husbands reveal the economic and social considerations that governed aristocratic marriages
- **Travel in the 1870s**: Marie's journey from Paris to Nice provides insights into train travel during this period
- **Aristocratic Leisure**: Activities like promenades, visits to the Bois de Boulogne, and dining out illustrate typical pastimes of the wealthy

## Marie's Personal Development

- **Self-Image**: Continued preoccupation with her appearance and how others perceive her
- **Romantic Idealism**: Elaborate fantasies about the Duke of Hamilton, despite having no actual relationship with him
- **Intellectual Ambitions**: Plans to resume studies in Nice with an ambitious schedule of nine hours of lessons per day
- **Philosophical Reflections**: Surprisingly mature reflections on love, jealousy, and marriage in the August 31 entry

## Setting Details

- **Paris**: Descriptions of fashionable areas, shops, and cultural venues in the French capital
- **Train Journey**: Observations of Mâcon, Marseille, Toulon, and other stops on the route from Paris to Nice
- **Nice**: Marie's return to her family home, with mentions of local landmarks like Monte-Carlo and the Var

## Significant Quotes

- "Tous les endroits sont égaux pour moi excepté Paris que j'adore et Nice que j'abhorre." (August 28)
- "Ah ! comme je voudrais être sa femme." (August 31)
- "Je ne puis épouser un homme pauvre, c'est contre ma nature, ce serait le malheur des deux." (August 31)
- "Je méprise la jalousie. Si un homme qui m'aime pense à une autre femme, ça veut dire qu'il ne m'aime plus, et il est ridicule d'être jalouse, ou de reprocher cela à l'homme." (August 31)

## Connections to Previous Months

The August entries continue Marie's infatuation with the Duke of Hamilton that began in January-February 1873. Her return to Nice brings her back to the social environment where she first encountered the Duke, though now with the added complication that her feelings for him have become known to her family. The month ends with Marie preparing to resume her studies in Nice, setting up a new phase in her daily routine.

## 1873-09

# September 1873: Studies Resume in Nice

## Overview

September 1873 finds Marie Bashkirtseff settling back into life in Nice after her Paris trip. The month is characterized by her resumption of an ambitious educational program, continued preoccupation with the Duke of Hamilton, family tensions, and social activities. Marie's diary entries reveal her complex personality: intellectually ambitious, socially conscious, romantically idealistic, and often frustrated by family dynamics. The entries provide a window into the life of a privileged young woman in the 1870s, balancing education, social obligations, and romantic fantasies.

## Main Topics and Themes

### Educational Pursuits
- **Ambitious Study Schedule**: Marie resumes an intensive educational program of nine hours daily
- **Multiple Languages**: Studies English, French, Italian, Latin, and German
- **Diverse Subjects**: Takes lessons in piano, drawing, literature, arithmetic, cosmography, and other sciences
- **Intellectual Pride**: Shows satisfaction in her academic progress and teacher praise

### Romantic Obsession
- **Duke of Hamilton**: Continued infatuation, with family members frequently teasing her about it
- **Romantic Fantasies**: Elaborate daydreams about marriage to the Duke
- **Gioia**: Ongoing concern about the Duke's relationship with this woman
- **Marriage Philosophy**: Sophisticated reflections on age differences, infidelity, and marriage expectations

### Family Dynamics
- **Household Tensions**: Frequent misunderstandings and arguments with family members
- **Mother-Daughter Relationship**: Complex interactions with her mother, including disagreements about household management
- **Family Teasing**: Continued jokes about Marie's interest in the Duke of Hamilton
- **Social Propriety**: Strong reactions to perceived improprieties, especially regarding her brother Georges

### Social Life
- **Theater Attendance**: Detailed account of the opening of the French Theater season
- **Gambling at Monte-Carlo**: Social outings to the casino
- **Household Staff**: Negotiations and decisions about coachmen and carriages
- **Visitors**: Regular social calls from family acquaintances and friends

## Key Events

1. **September 1-2**: Marie's return to Nice is complete, with family conversations dominated by teasing about the Duke of Hamilton

7. **September 13**: Attendance at the opening of the French Theater season

- **Walitsky**: Family friend who teases Marie about the Duke
- **Princess** (referred to as "la Bête"): A family friend who encourages Marie's fantasies about the Duke

- **Aristocratic Leisure**: Activities like theater attendance, gambling at Monte-Carlo, and carriage rides
- **Fashion Consciousness**: Detailed descriptions of clothing and orders from Worth in Paris

- **Self-Image Concerns**: Continued preoccupation with appearance and how others perceive her
- **Emotional Maturity**: Surprisingly sophisticated reflections on marriage and relationships
- **Social Awareness**: Acute observations of family dynamics and social improprieties

## Setting Details

- **Nice**: Family home and social environment, with references to local landmarks

- **French Theater**: Detailed description of the theater and performances
- **Household Spaces**: References to Marie's bedroom, the dining room, and other domestic spaces

## Significant Quotes

- "Je ne pourrais pas vivre pauvre. Mais si... si ça se pouvait, quel bonheur !" (September 10)

Marie's complex feelings about Nice itself—expressed in August as "Nice que j'abhorre" (Nice that I abhor)—are reflected in her frequent frustrations with family life there.
