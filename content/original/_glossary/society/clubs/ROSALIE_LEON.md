# Rosalie Léon

**Research Status**: Basic
**Last Updated**: 2025-07-05
**Diary Coverage**: Up to 1874-03-21

## Identity
Woman in Nice society. Originally thought to be fictional name for Wittgenstein in "Les Décavés," but appears to be real person.

## Diary References

### 1874
- **January 27**: Marie notes that in "Les Décavés," Wittgenstein is called Rosalie Léon.
- **March 21**: Seen as real person: "Cette détestable Rosalie Léon se promène en coupé avec une petite fille de cinq à six ans. Elle est laide et vieille et à un air pincé et la face couverte de *taches de rousseur*" (This detestable Rosalie Léon rides in a coupé with a little girl of 5-6 years. She is ugly and old with a pinched air and face covered with freckles).

## Context
- Initially appears as fictional name in novel
- Actually real person Marie dislikes
- Has child or ward aged 5-6
- Drives in coupé (sign of wealth)
- Marie finds her detestable, ugly, old
- Freckled face and pinched expression
