# Les Mirlitons

**Research Status**: Basic
**Last Updated**: 2025-01-23
**Diary Coverage**: Up to 1881-02-07

An art gallery or exhibition space in Paris where contemporary art was displayed in the early 1880s. Marie visits on February 7, 1881, to see portraits by Carolus-Duran and Bastien-Lepage.

## Diary References
- February 7, 1881: Marie visits with Villevieille and Brisbane to see portraits, including Carolus-Duran's masterpiece of a woman with a little girl in red, and Bastien-Lepage's portrait of the Prince of Wales

## Notable Exhibitions
- Carolus-Duran: Portrait of woman alone (older, made-up) in plum-colored Louis XIII dress
- Carolus-Duran: Woman with child in red
- Bastien-Lepage: Portrait of Prince of Wales in Henri VII costume with Thames and English fleet background
- Saint-Marceaux: Terra cotta sculpture
