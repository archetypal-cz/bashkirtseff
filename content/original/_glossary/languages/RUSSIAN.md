# Russian

Marie Bashkirtseff's native language, used in the diary for:

- **Family terms**: "diadia" (uncle), diminutives, patronymics
- **Domestic contexts**: Household matters, servants
- **Russian culture**: Orthodox traditions, Russian customs
- **Intimate moments**: Mother-daughter conversations, childhood memories

## Common Russian Words in Marie's Diary

- "Moussia" - Marie's family nickname (diminutive of Maria)
- "diadia" - uncle
- Patronymics for Russian relatives and acquaintances
- Terms for Russian Orthodox religious practices

## Usage Notes for Translators

Russian code-switching often signals:
- Intimacy and family context
- Connection to Russian identity
- Childhood/domestic sphere
- Religious/cultural traditions
