[^82.454.1]: Princess Thyra of Denmark (1853-1933) was rumored in 1878 as a potential bride for the Prince Imperial (Napoleon Eugene Louis Jean Joseph Bonaparte, 1856-1879), commonly called Napoleon IV by Bonapartists. The match never materialized; Thyra married Ernest Augustus, Crown Prince of Hanover, in December 1878. The Prince Imperial died the following year in the Anglo-Zulu War.

[^82.462.1]: The Academie Julian was a private art school in Paris founded in 1868 by painter Rodolphe Julian. It was one of the few schools that accepted women and allowed them to draw from the nude male model, making it crucial for aspiring female artists like Marie who were barred from the official Ecole des Beaux-Arts.
