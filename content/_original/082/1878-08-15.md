# Jeudi, 15 août 1878

%% 082.0086 %%
%% [#Soden](../_glossary/places/cities/SODEN.md) [#Schwarz](../_glossary/people/mentioned/SCHWARZ.md) [#Dourassoff](../_glossary/people/mentioned/DOURASSOFF.md) [#Mme_Abaza](../_glossary/people/aristocracy/MME_ABAZA.md) [#Comte_Doenhoff](../_glossary/people/aristocracy/COMTE_DOENHOFF.md) [#Prince_Auguste_de_Hohenlohe_Oehringen](../_glossary/people/aristocracy/PRINCE_AUGUSTE_DE_HOHENLOHE_OEHRINGEN.md) [#Ma_tante](../_glossary/people/family/MA_TANTE.md) [#Maman](../_glossary/people/family/MAMAN.md) [#Grand_papa](../_glossary/people/family/GRAND_PAPA.md) %%
%% 2026-02-09T10:30:00 RSR: August 15 is the Feast of the Assumption, a major Catholic holiday. Marie's "je suis chretienne helas!" (I am a Christian, alas!) is ironic - her Christianity prevents her from taking revenge on the princes after the humiliation of the previous day. %%

%% 082.0087 %%
%% 2026-02-02T14:00:00 LAN: "chrétienne hélas !" - Christian alas! - ironic regret about Christian forgiveness preventing revenge %%
Je suis chrétienne hélas !

%% 082.0088 %%
%% 2026-02-02T14:00:00 LAN: "accosté" - accosted/approached, implies deliberate confrontation %%
%% 2026-02-02T14:00:00 LAN: "malentendu" - misunderstanding, diplomatic euphemism for social disaster %%
%% 2026-02-02T14:00:00 LAN: "faire connaissance" - make acquaintance, formal introduction process %%
%% 2026-02-02T14:00:00 LAN: "ôter votre chapeau" - remove your hat, etiquette requirement when addressing ladies %%
%% 2026-02-09T10:30:00 RSR: Marie's calculated final confrontation with Schwarz (Prince Auguste de Hohenlohe-Oehringen). She turns his rejection into her own: "If Dourassoff had consulted me, I wouldn't have wanted your acquaintance either." Her etiquette lecture about hat-tipping when speaking to a lady uses aristocratic protocol as a weapon. This reveals his identity - she later writes to Count Doenhoff to gather intelligence on "Prince Auguste de Hohenlohe-Oehringen." The prince is August Karl Prinz zu Hohenlohe-Oehringen (b. January 2, 1854, d. January 27, 1884 in San Remo), son of Hugo, 4th Prince of Hohenlohe-Oehringen, Duke of Ujest. His brother (Marie's "Gans") is likely Friedrich Prinz zu Hohenlohe-Oehringen (b. September 21, 1855). Auguste's death at San Remo (a Mediterranean resort for consumptives) at age 30 confirms Marie's observation of his "sickly skin" - he too suffered from tuberculosis, as did Marie herself. %%
J'ai fait ce que je voulais, j'ai rencontré Schwarz et je l'ai accosté. Monsieur, lui dis-je, hier il est arrivé un désagréable malentendu; M. Dourassoff a voulu vous présenter croyant bien faire; vous ne l'avez pas voulu. Et je viens vous dire que si M. Dourassoff m'avait consultée c'est moi qui n'aurait pas voulu. Nous faisons connaissance entre Russes mais quant aux étrangers c'est différent. Je n'ai pas la moindre envie de faire votre connaissance. Et quand vous parlerez une autre fois à une dame, je vous conseille d'ôter votre chapeau.

%% 082.0089 %%
%% 2026-02-02T14:00:00 LAN: "yeux myopes" - myopic/short-sighted eyes, revealed without flattering pince-nez %%
%% 2026-02-02T14:00:00 LAN: "peau maladive" - sickly skin, tuberculosis indicator %%
%% 2026-02-09T10:30:00 RSR: Without the blue pince-nez that made his eyes look beautiful, Marie now sees Auguste clearly: myopic eyes, thin cheeks, sickly skin. The romantic illusion shatters. He defends himself saying it was Dourassoff's fault and that "among us [Germans] it's also not the custom" to make unsolicited introductions. %%
Il avait l'air tout à fait en colère, ces vilains yeux myopes qui paraissent si beaux sous des verres bleus, je les ai bien vus et ces joues maigres et cette peau maladive. Plusieurs fois il a interrompu pour dire que c'est la faute de M. Dourassoff que "chez nous non plus ce n'est pas l'usage", qu'il ne voulait pas nous déranger etc.

%% 082.0090 %%
%% 2026-02-02T14:00:00 LAN: "impertinences" - impertinent remarks, social rudeness to social superiors %%
%% 2026-02-02T14:00:00 LAN: "tout bonnement" - quite simply/frankly, intensifier %%
%% 2026-02-02T14:00:00 LAN: "scandale" - scandal, social disgrace with public consequences %%
%% 2026-02-02T14:00:00 LAN: "Allemand borné" - narrow-minded German, national stereotype %%
%% 2026-02-02T14:00:00 LAN: "correction" - correction/punishment, divine discipline %%
%% 2026-02-09T10:30:00 RSR: The princes escalate by threatening through Dourassoff that they will "take revenge" if Marie makes more "impertinent remarks." This crosses from social snub into genuine threat, creating a real scandal. Marie's national stereotyping ("imagining a narrow-minded German to be an intelligent Frenchman") reflects the Franco-German tensions still raw after the 1870-71 war. Her grandfather's declining health in Paris provides the urgent pretext for departure. %%
Ensuite, pendant que je prenais le thé avec les autres ils ont appelé Dourassoff et lui ont dit en termes grossiers que si je leur disais encore des impertinences ils se vengeraient. Dourassoff est venu me le répéter. Bref c'est tout bonnement un scandale. Quelle idée aussi de s'imaginer qu'un Allemand borné soit un Français intelligent. Je suis excessivement si dérangée par tout cela que je tremble de colère et de honte... quand je vous disais que Mon Dieu viendrait à mon secours pour m'appliquer une correction quelconque...

%% 082.0091 %%
Dourassoff ira le raconter à tout le monde. Je voudrais être je ne sais où..

[^82.490.1]: The prince Marie confronted is August Karl Prinz zu Hohenlohe-Oehringen (1854-1884), son of Hugo, 4th Prince of Hohenlohe-Oehringen and Duke of Ujest. The Hohenlohe-Oehringen were a prominent German princely family with vast estates in Silesia and Wurttemberg. Auguste died at age 30 in San Remo, likely of tuberculosis -- the same disease affecting Marie, who noted his "sickly skin" and "suffering eyes." His brother, likely Friedrich (b. 1855), is the one Marie nicknamed "Gans."
