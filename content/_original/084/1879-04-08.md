%% 2026-02-09T22:07:00 RSR: "La Dame de Montsoreau" — theatrical adaptation of Alexandre Dumas pere's 1846 novel, set during the French Wars of Religion. The play was adapted by Dumas and Auguste Maquet, and was a staple of boulevard theater. The Theatre de la Porte Saint-Martin, on the Boulevard Saint-Martin, was one of Paris's most important theaters for romantic melodrama. Marie is enchanted by the swashbuckling world of the play — gentlemen, duels, ambushes, love affairs — and explicitly compares Cassagnac to Bussy d'Amboise (Louis de Clermont d'Amboise, seigneur de Bussy, 1549-1579), the romantic hero of the novel. This reveals how Marie mythologizes the men she admires. %%

Je n'y pense nullement. Je me serais peut-etre trouvee dans sa position... mais c'est la piece qui est adorable.
