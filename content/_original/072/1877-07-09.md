%% 2026-02-09T20:00:00 RSR: Famous fortune-telling scene. Dona Estephana, a Portuguese cartomancer (card-reader), provides an extended reading that Marie transcribes "mot pour mot." The reading is remarkably accurate: she identifies a troublesome young man (Alexandre/Larderei), a "petit homme" who causes trouble (probably Melissano or Torlonia), a serious older man of 30-40 who will offer honorable marriage, three imagined passions, artistic ability, and living past 90 (tragically wrong -- Marie died at 25). Marie's numbered footnotes decode the reading: 1=Melissano or Torlonia, 2=Monaco disguise (the "coup de tete"), 3=Audiffret/Antonelli/Alexandre (her "three passions"). The "Aquarium" at Monaco refers to the Monaco casino episode. Later at the Bois, Marie spots Cassagnac (Paul de Cassagnac, the Bonapartist journalist) for the first time in Paris -- he recognizes and salutes her. The fortune-teller's prediction that Alexandre doesn't love her sends Marie into distress, ending with a defiant declaration: "je l'aurai quand meme!" The Hotel du Louvre reference recalls earlier Paris miseries. %%
