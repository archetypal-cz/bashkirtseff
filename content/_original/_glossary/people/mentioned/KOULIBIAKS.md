# Koulibiaks

**Research Status**: Basic
**Last Updated**: 2025-07-06
**Diary Coverage**: Up to 1874-03-05

## Identity
Russian savory pies (кулебяка/kulebyaka), traditional festive food ordered by Marie for the villa blessing ceremony.

## Diary References

### 1874
- **March 5**: Marie orders "des koulibiaks etc." for the villa blessing ceremony the next day.

## Context
- Traditional Russian filled pastry
- Rectangular pies with layers of filling
- Common fillings: salmon, rice, eggs, mushrooms, cabbage
- Served at celebrations and special occasions
- Marie's use of "etc." suggests other Russian foods ordered
- Shows family maintaining Russian culinary traditions

## Cultural Significance
- Symbol of Russian hospitality
- Complex dish showing wealth and sophistication
- Appropriate for religious house blessing ceremony
- Part of maintaining Russian identity in exile
