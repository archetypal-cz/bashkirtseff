## Basic Information

%% GLO_MARGHERITA_DI_SAVOIA.0002 %%
Margherita Maria Teresa Giovanna di Savoia (20 November 1851 -- 4 January 1926) was the Queen consort of Italy as the wife of King Umberto I, who reigned from 1878 to 1900. She was widely admired for her beauty, elegance, and patronage of the arts.

## Connection to Marie's Diary

%% GLO_MARGHERITA_DI_SAVOIA.0004 %%
Marie spent significant time in Italy (Rome, Naples, Florence) during 1877-1878, and Queen Margherita was a prominent public figure during this period. As a cultured queen who patronized art and artists, Margherita represented the kind of elevated female influence Marie admired. Her name appears in Marie's diary in the context of Italian social life and events.

## Historical Note

%% GLO_MARGHERITA_DI_SAVOIA.0006 %%
The famous pizza Margherita is named after her, created in her honor during a visit to Naples in 1889.
