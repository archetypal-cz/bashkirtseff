# Alexander Pushkin

**Research Status**: Basic
**Last Updated**: 2025-12-07
**Diary Coverage**: Book 00 (1884 preface)

## Identity

Alexander Sergeyevich Pushkin (1799-1837) was Russia's greatest poet and the founder of modern Russian literature. Marie mentions him as a contemporary of her maternal grandfather.

## Biography

**Life and Career:**
- Born 1799 in Moscow to noble family
- Considered the father of Russian literary language
- Master of poetry, prose, drama, and historical writing
- Major works include "Eugene Onegin," "The Bronze Horseman," "Boris Godunov"
- Combined Romantic sensibility with emerging Realism

**Death:**
- Killed in a duel in 1837 at age 37
- Defending his wife's honor against Georges d'Anthès
- His death shocked Russian society
- Lermontov's poem denouncing the court for Pushkin's death made Lermontov famous

## Cultural Significance

Pushkin's importance to Russian culture cannot be overstated:
- Created the foundation of modern Russian literary language
- Influenced every Russian writer who followed
- Symbol of Russian cultural identity
- His works are memorized by Russian schoolchildren
- June 6 (his birthday) is celebrated as Russian Language Day

## Pushkin's Generation

The generation of the 1820s-1830s:
- Influenced by the Napoleonic Wars (1812 invasion of Russia)
- Witnessed the Decembrist Revolt (1825)
- Embraced European Romanticism while developing Russian themes
- Combined aristocratic privilege with liberal sympathies
- Often faced censorship and exile for political views

## Connection to Bashkirtseff Family

Marie's maternal grandfather was "le contemporain de Lermontoff, Pouchkine etc." This identifies him as:
- Member of educated Russian nobility (1820s-1840s)
- Cultured and literary ("militaire lettré")
- Part of the Romantic generation
- Likely familiar with Pushkin's poetry
- Representative of Russia's "Golden Age" of literature

This cultural context was important to Marie's understanding of her family heritage.
