## Thematic Tag: Death & Funerals

%% GLO_DEATH.0002 %%
This thematic tag collects diary paragraphs describing literal death events: funerals, burials, mourning periods, and encounters with the dead. Distinguished from the broader Mortality theme, which captures Marie's figurative and philosophical preoccupation with death.

%% GLO_DEATH.0003 %%
## Key Patterns

%% GLO_DEATH.0004 %%
- **Funerals and burials**: Marie attends or describes enterrements, pompes funebres, pannychides (Orthodox funeral vigils)
- **Mourning**: Deuil as both social obligation and fashion statement ("je porte un deuil ravissant")
- **Deaths of acquaintances**: Abramovitch (cholera, 1873), Fedoroff (aneurysm, 1874), and others
- **Tombs and cemeteries**: Visits to tombeaux, sepulcres, cimetieres

%% GLO_DEATH.0005 %%
## Marie's Death Vocabulary

%% GLO_DEATH.0006 %%
- "pannychide" — Orthodox funeral vigil (Russian cultural term)
- "pompes funebres" — undertakers/funeral services
- "passe de vie a trepas" — euphemism: "passed from life to death"
- "dernier soupir" — last breath
- "deuil ravissant" — beautiful mourning clothes (Marie's aesthetic take on death)
