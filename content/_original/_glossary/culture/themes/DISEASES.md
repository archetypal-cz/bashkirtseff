## Thematic Tag: Diseases & Diagnoses

%% GLO_DISEASES.0002 %%
This thematic tag collects diary paragraphs mentioning specific named diseases and medical diagnoses. Complements the broader Health theme by focusing on identified illnesses rather than general symptoms, doctors, or treatment.

%% GLO_DISEASES.0003 %%
## Diseases in the Diary

%% GLO_DISEASES.0004 %%
- **Phtisie / phtisie laryngee**: Marie's primary illness — tuberculosis in its period terminology. She never uses "tuberculose." First fears appear in 1875; confirmed diagnosis comes gradually through the 1880s.
- **Cholera**: The 1873 Vienna epidemic affects travel plans; Abramovitch dies of cholera in Nice (August 1873)
- **Fievre typhoide / typhus**: Mentioned in family illness contexts and Marie's occasional dark wish to fall ill
- **Laryngite**: Marie's persistent throat condition, diagnosed from 1878 onward
- **Pleurisie / pleuresie**: Lung inflammation, diagnosed 1881; "l'enveloppe du poumon droit est malade"
- **Bronchite / peri-bronchite**: Recurring respiratory diagnosis, euphemistic alternative to phtisie
- **Catarrhe**: Nasal/throat inflammation, early diagnosis
- **Variole**: Smallpox — mentioned once in a prayer ("delivre-nous de la variole")
- **Anemie**: Dismissed diagnosis — "il m'examine et declare une anemie. C'est un imbecile."
- **Anevrisme**: Cause of Fedoroff's death (1874)

%% GLO_DISEASES.0005 %%
## Marie's Diagnostic Language

%% GLO_DISEASES.0006 %%
Marie often records diagnoses with skepticism or dark humor:
- "Pharyngite, laryngite et catarrhe. Rien que cela." — Nothing but that.
- "la fameuse peri-bronchite chronique" — the famous chronic peri-bronchitis (euphemism)
- "Je voudrais bien que ma phtisie fut imaginaire." — I wish my tuberculosis were imaginary.
