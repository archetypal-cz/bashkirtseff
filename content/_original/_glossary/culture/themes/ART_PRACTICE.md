## Thematic Tag: Art Practice

%% GLO_ART_PRACTICE.0002 %%
This thematic tag collects diary paragraphs where Marie discusses her own artistic work — painting, drawing, studio practice, exhibitions, artistic ambition, and the technical and emotional process of making art.

%% GLO_ART_PRACTICE.0003 %%
## Marie's Artistic Life

%% GLO_ART_PRACTICE.0004 %%
- **Pre-Julian (1873-1877)**: Drawing lessons, copying, amateur sketching; art as accomplishment rather than vocation
- **Académie Julian (1877-1884)**: Intensive professional training; daily atelier work, competitions (concours), critiques from masters
- **Salon submissions**: Preparation of works for the annual Salon, the anxiety of jury acceptance, and public exhibition
- **Late period (1882-1884)**: Racing against illness to create lasting work; "Le Meeting" and other major paintings

%% GLO_ART_PRACTICE.0005 %%
## Common Topics

%% GLO_ART_PRACTICE.0006 %%
- **Studio work**: Drawing from the model, painting studies, academic exercises
- **Concours**: Weekly competitions at the Académie Julian; Marie's obsessive tracking of results
- **Teacher critiques**: Robert-Fleury's and Lefebvre's evaluations of her work
- **Self-assessment**: Marie's brutal honesty about her own abilities and progress
- **Artistic ambition**: The drive for recognition, medals, Salon acceptance
- **Art as destiny**: Marie's conviction that art is her purpose and legacy
- **Rivalry**: Competition with other women students, especially Breslau

%% GLO_ART_PRACTICE.0007 %%
## Marie's Artistic Vocabulary

%% GLO_ART_PRACTICE.0008 %%
- **Étude** — study (academic exercise)
- **Esquisse** — sketch, preliminary drawing
- **Ébauche** — rough draft of a painting
- **Concours** — competition
- **Le modèle** — the model (live model)
- **L'atelier** — the studio
