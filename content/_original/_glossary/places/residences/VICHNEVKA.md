# Vichnevka (Vishnevka)

**Research Status**: Basic
**Last Updated**: 2026-02-10
**Diary Coverage**: Family references

## Overview

Vichnevka (Vishnevka, from "vishnya" meaning cherry) was likely one of the Bashkirtseff family properties or a neighboring estate in the Poltava region of Ukraine. The name, derived from the Russian/Ukrainian word for cherry, was typical of estate names in the agricultural regions of the Russian Empire, where properties were often named after natural features or local flora.

## Context

The Bashkirtseff family's Ukrainian estates:
- Located in the Poltava governorate of the Russian Empire
- Agricultural properties worked by serfs (before 1861) and then by freed peasants
- The source of the family's wealth, providing income even while they lived abroad
- Managed in the family's absence by stewards or relatives
- Marie's father, Konstantin Bashkirtseff, remained connected to the estate management

## Significance for Marie

- **Family property**: Part of the network of estates supporting the family's lifestyle
- **Ukrainian roots**: Connected to the homeland Marie left as a child
- **Economic basis**: The agricultural income from such estates funded the family's European life
