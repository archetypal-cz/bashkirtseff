%% 2025-12-06T14:30:00 LAN: "au Salon" - the annual Paris art exhibition (Salon de peinture et de sculpture), NOT a living room. THE major art event of 1880s Paris where reputations were made. %%
%% 2025-12-06T14:30:00 LAN: "charmante" - period self-assessment of appearance/presentation; implies elegant, pleasing to look at; social approval term, not mere vanity %%
%% 2025-12-06T14:30:00 LAN: "faux air de frondeuse" - complex expression: "faux air" = a slight resemblance/manner suggesting; "frondeuse" = female rebel (from La Fronde, 1648-1653 civil war). Marie sees herself as elegantly defiant. Translate the rebellious spirit, not literally. %%
%% 2025-12-06T00:00:00 RSR: "La Reine" = Queen Isabel II of Spain at her Palais de Castille - royalist/Bonapartist salon on Avenue Kléber. Marie went hoping to meet Cassagnac, the Bonapartist journalist she admired. %%
%% 2025-12-06T00:00:00 RSR: "faux air de frondeuse" - Marie describes herself looking like a rebel/resister, a stylish self-assessment. The Fronde was the 17th-century French civil war, so "frondeuse" suggests elegant defiance. %%
Nous allons chez la Reine avec maman et puis au Salon avec Villevieille. Je suis charmante en gris, faux air de frondeuse.
