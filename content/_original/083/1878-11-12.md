# Mardi, 12 novembre 1878

%% 083.0143 %%
%% [#Kernberger](../_glossary/people/writers/KATHERINE_KERNBERGER.md) %%
%% [#Paris](../_glossary/places/cities/PARIS.md) [#Mme_Yorke](../_glossary/people/aristocracy/MME_YORKE.md) %%
%% 2026-02-02T13:00:00 LAN: "foudroyante" - thunderstruck/devastating; shocking sudden news %%
%% 2026-02-02T13:00:00 LAN: "En pleine vie" - in full life; in prime of life, unexpectedly %%
%% 2026-02-09T13:16:00 RSR: Mme Yorke - a woman from Marie's social circle, dead suddenly at barely 32. The shock of mortality among the young and carefree ("so gay, so carefree!") hits Marie viscerally. %%
Aujourd'hui nous apprenons une nouvelle foudroyante. Mme Yorke est morte. En pleine vie, âgée de trente-deux ans à peine, si gaie, si insouciante !

%% 083.0144 %%
%% 2026-02-02T13:00:00 LAN: "congestion au cerveau" - brain congestion; 19th-c. medical term for stroke/cerebral hemorrhage %%
%% 2026-02-09T13:16:00 RSR: 19th-century medical understanding: chronic migraines leading to "brain congestion" (cerebral hemorrhage/stroke). A common but poorly understood cause of sudden death in the period. %%
Cette pauvre Yorke est morte sans maladie, elle n'a jamais souffert que des migraines, ce sont ces migraines je crois qui ont déterminé la congestion au cerveau qui l'a emportée.

%% 083.0145 %%
%% [#Julian](../_glossary/places/schools/JULIAN.md) [#Pere_Cassagnac](../_glossary/people/mentioned/PERE_CASSAGNAC.md) %%
%% 2026-02-02T13:00:00 LAN: "en deux coups de crayon" - in two pencil strokes; idiom for rapid, skilled sketching %%
%% 2026-02-09T13:16:00 RSR: Marie's artistic breakthrough moment at the evening class - she dashes off a quick sketch of "the most extraordinary head in France" next to her academic study. The subject is Granier de Cassagnac pere (Paul-Adolphe, 1806-1880), Paul de Cassagnac's father, himself a prominent journalist and politician. %%
Ce soir je dessinai en deux coups de crayon à côté de mon académie la tête la plus extraordinaire qu'il y ait en France.

%% 083.0146 %%
%% [#Balzac](../_glossary/culture/literature/BALZAC.md) %%
%% 2026-02-02T13:00:00 LAN: "Jacques Collin" - Balzac character Vautrin/Abbe Herrera; criminal mastermind with dual identity %%
%% 2026-02-02T13:00:00 LAN: "grandiose et ignoble" - grandiose and ignoble; oxymoronic characterization %%
%% 2026-02-09T13:16:00 RSR: Marie's literary-artistic insight: she compares pere Cassagnac to Jacques Collin (alias Vautrin, alias Abbe Carlos Herrera), the great criminal mastermind of Balzac's Comedie humaine. The "grandiose and ignoble" oxymoron perfectly captures Balzac's ambivalent characterization - and Marie's sophisticated reading of both literature and physiognomy. %%
— C'est une tête grandiose et ignoble, repris-je, c'est Jacques Collin.

%% 083.0147 %%
%% [#Pere_Cassagnac](../_glossary/people/mentioned/PERE_CASSAGNAC.md) %%
%% 2026-02-02T13:00:00 LAN: "mansarde" - garret; attic room, often poor artist/bohemian housing %%
%% 2026-02-02T13:00:00 LAN: "Abbé Herrera" - another alias for Vautrin in Balzac's novels %%
%% 2026-02-09T13:16:00 RSR: Marie envisions a painting combining Balzac's literary world with real-life models: the "Jewish woman's garret" scene from Splendeurs et miseres des courtisanes, with Heilbronn (likely a fellow student) as the Jewish woman and pere Cassagnac as Abbe Herrera/Vautrin. She declares such a painting would demonstrate "genius" - this is Marie's artistic conception at its most ambitious, fusing literary imagination with portraiture. %%
Le peintre qui ferait un tableau représentant la mansarde de la juive avec la juive, sous les traits d'Heilbronn et l'abbé Herrera avec le père de Cassagnac pour modèle, aura plus que du talent, il aura du génie.
