%% 083.0675 %%
# Jeudi, 2 janvier 1879

%% 083.1139 %%
%% [#Paris](../_glossary/places/cities/PARIS.md) [#Tuileries](../_glossary/places/parks/TUILERIES.md) [#Luxembourg](../_glossary/places/parks/LUXEMBOURG.md) %%
%% 2026-02-09T15:06:00 RSR: One of Marie's most famous feminist passages. She catalogs the specific freedoms denied to women: walking alone, coming and going freely, sitting on park benches, stopping at artistic shopfronts, entering churches and museums, walking in old streets at night. The conclusion: "that is the freedom without which one cannot become a great artist." This is not abstract feminism but lived experience of concrete restrictions. %%
Ce que j'envie c'est la liberté de se promener tout seul, d'aller, de venir, de s'asseoir sur les bancs des jardins des Tuileries et surtout du Luxembourg, de s'arrêter aux vitrines artistiques, d'entrer dans les églises, les musées, de se promener lé soir dans les vieilles rues, voilà ce que j'envie et voilà la liberté sans laquelle on ne pas devenir un grand artiste.

%% 083.1140 %%
%% 2026-02-02T13:00:00 LAN: "cré nom d'un chien" - damn it (lit. 'created name of a dog'); euphemism for sacred name blasphemy %%
%% 2026-02-02T13:00:00 LAN: "habits bourgeois" - bourgeois/plain clothes; unremarkable masculine attire for disguise %%
%% 2026-02-09T15:06:00 RSR: The radical solution: disguise in "bourgeois clothes and a wig" to make herself so ugly she will be "free as a man." This echoes George Sand's famous masculine disguise in Paris decades earlier. Marie's rage at being a woman erupts with the oath "cre nom d'un chien" (a euphemistic blasphemy). %%
Ah ! cré nom d'un chien. Voilà quand je rage d'être femme. Je vais m'arranger. Des habits bourgeois et une perruque, je me ferai si laide que je serai libre comme un homme.

%% 083.1141 %%
%% 2026-02-02T13:00:00 LAN: "crasse ignorance" - crass/filthy ignorance; strong condemnation of social prejudice %%
%% 2026-02-02T13:00:00 LAN: "sauvage routine" - savage routine; social conventions as barbaric constraint %%
%% 2026-02-09T15:06:00 RSR: Marie identifies the systemic cause: "This is one of the great reasons why there are no women artists." Her twin condemnation - "crass ignorance" and "savage routine" - attacks both the intellectual poverty and the entrenched social customs that constrain women. This anticipates by a century Linda Nochlin's famous essay "Why Have There Been No Great Women Artists?" (1971). %%
C'est une des grandes raisons pour lesquelles il n'y a pas d'artistes femmes. Oh ! crasse ignorance, ô sauvage routine.

%% 083.1142 %%
%% 2026-02-09T15:06:00 RSR: Marie's artistic mission statement: she will prove to society what a woman can become "despite all the disadvantages heaped upon her by society." The word "quelque chose" (something) is deliberately modest - not specifying greatness but simply demonstrating female capability against systemic obstacles. %%
Moi je tâcherai de la donner la société en lui montrant une femme quelque chose malgré tous les désavantages dont la comble la société.
