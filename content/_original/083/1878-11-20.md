# Mercredi, 20 novembre 1878

%% 083.1002 %%
%% [#Paris](../_glossary/places/cities/PARIS.md) %%
%% 2026-02-09T13:34:00 RSR: Marie's post-bath beauty revelation. The twenty minutes of self-contemplation show the painter's eye turned on herself - she becomes both subject and observer. %%
Ce soir après mon bain je suis devenue subitement si jolie que j'ai passé vingt minutes à me regarder.

%% 083.1003 %%
%% 2026-02-09T13:34:00 RSR: Marie's precise artistic analysis of her own coloring: "absolutely dazzling color, so fine, so gentle, barely rose cheeks; for vigor only the lips and eyebrows with the eyes." This is the painter's palette applied to self-portraiture - the delicate overall tone contrasted with bold accents. %%
Une couleur absolument éblouissante et si fine, si douce, les joues à peine rosées; en fait de vigueur il n'y a que les lèvres et les sourcils avec les yeux.

%% 083.1004 %%
%% 2026-02-02T13:00:00 LAN: "se parcheminer" - to become parchment-like; aging, drying of skin %%
%% 2026-02-09T13:34:00 RSR: Beauty recognized as useless in current isolation ("serves nothing") but potentially valuable in the future ("will serve"). The horror at inevitable decay - "must fade, become parchment-like, and die" - is both vanity and mortality anxiety. Marie, who already shows early symptoms of what will eventually kill her (tuberculosis), has particular reason for urgency about transient beauty. %%
Et dire que cela ne sert à rien ! Cela servira... mais ce qui est infâme, c'est que tout cela doit se faner, se parcheminer et mourir !

%% 083.1005 %%
%% 2026-02-09T13:34:00 RSR: Four words that mark a turning point: "Painting takes everything." Art is becoming the consuming force in Marie's life, displacing (if not fully replacing) the Cassagnac obsession. %%
La peinture prend tout.
