# Vendredi, 8 novembre 1878

%% 083.0123 %%
%% [#Paris](../_glossary/places/cities/PARIS.md) [#Versailles](../_glossary/places/cities/VERSAILLES.md) [#Gambetta](../_glossary/people/politicians/GAMBETTA.md) [#Mme_Gavini](../_glossary/people/mentioned/MME_GAVINI.md) %%
%% 2026-02-02T13:00:00 LAN: "cherubin de Gambetta" - cherub of a Gambetta; ironic affection for political opponent who provided tickets %%
%% 2026-02-09T12:54:00 RSR: Second Versailles trip. Gambetta (1838-1882), Republican leader and president of the Chamber, ironically called "cherub" by Marie despite being her Bonapartist friends' political opponent. Gavini obtained questors' box seats - a superior viewing position in parliament. %%
Hier nous allons donc à Versailles avec les billets de ce chérubin de Gambetta.

%% 083.0124 %%
%% [#Paul_de_Cassagnac](../_glossary/people/recurring/CASSAGNAC.md) [#Julia_Acard](../_glossary/people/mentioned/JULIA_ACARD.md) %%
%% 2026-02-09T12:54:00 RSR: Exchange between a colleague and Cassagnac praising his "talent and energy" - his gracious response calling it "the most flattering tribute I've ever received." Marie's pointed reference to his wife as "Mlle Julia Acard" (maiden name, not married name) shows deliberate disrespect. %%
— Beaucoup de talent et beaucoup d'énergie ! lui dit-il. — C'est l'hommage le plus flatteur que j'aie jamais reçu, répondit le mari de Mlle Julia Acard.

%% 083.0125 %%
%% [#Paul_de_Cassagnac](../_glossary/people/recurring/CASSAGNAC.md) %%
%% 2026-02-02T13:00:00 LAN: "fanee" - faded/withered; describing aging/worn appearance %%
%% 2026-02-02T13:00:00 LAN: "fletrie" - withered/wilted; more emphatic than "fanee" %%
%% 2026-02-09T12:54:00 RSR: Marie's cruel physical dissection of Julia Acard continues: "ordinary," "faded," "so brown, so black that her skin around the nostrils, chin and mouth appears withered." The artistic eye weaponized for jealous scrutiny. %%
Mon pauvre Cassagnac ! Elle est ordinaire ! Elle est fanée, peut-être même pas, mais si brune, si noire que sa peau du tour des narines, du menton et de la bouche paraît flétrie...

%% 083.0126 %%
%% [#Clemenceau](../_glossary/people/politicians/CLEMENCEAU.md) %%
%% 2026-02-09T12:54:00 RSR: Georges Clemenceau (1841-1929) was at this time a relatively new deputy (elected 1876), leader of the Radical Republicans. Marie's prescient observation that he is "very strong though relatively unknown" foreshadows his eventual rise to become one of France's most powerful politicians, including Prime Minister during WWI ("The Tiger"). %%
Il y a un homme qui est très fort quoique relativement inconnu, c'est Clémenceau.

%% 083.0127 %%
%% [#Rouher](../_glossary/people/politicians/ROUHER.md) [#Nice](../_glossary/places/cities/NICE.md) %%

%% 2026-02-09T12:54:00 RSR: Eugene Rouher (1814-1884), former Minister of State under Napoleon III, the most important Bonapartist politician. Marie's anecdote about "bonapartizing the grocer women and apple-sellers of Nice" amused the elder statesman. Meeting at Gare Saint-Lazare (the station for trains to Versailles) shows the informal networking of parliamentary culture. %%
C'est seulement à la gare Saint-Lazare que nous trouvons le père Rouher qui a été charmant pour moi et auquel j'ai raconté comment j'avais bonapartisé les épicières et les marchandes de pommes de Nice.
