# Samedi, 9 novembre 1878

%% 083.0129 %%
%% [#Paris](../_glossary/places/cities/PARIS.md) [#Julian](../_glossary/places/schools/JULIAN.md) [#Breslau](../_glossary/people/artists/BRESLAU_LOUISE.md) %%
%% 2026-02-02T13:00:00 LAN: "Une veste" - a jacket; colloquial: a defeat/failure, "taking a jacket" %%
%% 2026-02-02T13:00:00 LAN: "honteuse" - shameful; intensifying the defeat %%
%% 2026-02-09T13:10:00 RSR: Marie lost the Julian competition badly - no medal at all. The "imbecile women who are strong and didn't compete" likely refers to Breslau and other experienced students who abstained. Marie had been studying only one year vs Breslau's five, making the defeat predictable but still humiliating. %%
Une veste honteuse ! Pas de médaille du tout. Ce qui fera triompher ces imbéciles de femmes qui sont fortes et qui n'ont pas concouru.

%% 083.0130 %%
%% 2026-02-02T13:00:00 LAN: "en grande pompe" - with great pomp; ironically, in a grand manner %%
%% 2026-02-02T13:00:00 LAN: "académies" - academic figure studies; here meaning academic drawings from life %%
%% 2026-02-09T13:10:00 RSR: Marie rationalizes the defeat: she has only studied one year vs others' longer training, and the masters have told her she draws academic figures "as well as Breslau." This self-consolation through authority figures' praise is characteristic. %%
Donc nous avons un an. Tout cela pour vous annoncer en grande pompe que je dessine, les académies aussi bien que Breslau, les maîtres me l'ont dit.

%% 083.0131 %%
%% [#Paul_de_Cassagnac](../_glossary/people/recurring/CASSAGNAC.md) %%
%% 2026-02-02T13:00:00 LAN: "Popaul" - little Paul; affectionate/diminutive nickname for Paul de Cassagnac %%
%% 2026-02-09T13:10:00 RSR: Rumors circulating about both Marie's own marriage prospects and Cassagnac's potential separation from his wife. "Popaul" - the intimate diminutive shows the familiar tone in which Cassagnac is discussed in her social circle. The typographic emphasis on "ma-rie" in the original suggests playful wordplay. %%
Vous savez ce que l'on dit ? "On dit que l'on te m*arie*".. D'abord, et puis que Popaul se sépare d'avec son étrange épouse.

%% 083.0132 %%
%% [#Paul_de_Cassagnac](../_glossary/people/recurring/CASSAGNAC.md) %%
%% 2026-02-09T13:10:00 RSR: Dialogue about Julia Acard's appearance - a colleague offers the diplomatic "interesting face" assessment. %%
— Hum... pas précisément, mais c'est une figure intéressante.

%% 083.0133 %%
%% [#Mme_Gavini](../_glossary/people/mentioned/MME_GAVINI.md) [#Cardinal_Antonelli](../_glossary/people/mentioned/CARDINAL_ANTONELLI.md) %%
%% 2026-02-09T13:10:00 RSR: Gavini's cutting comparison of Julia Acard to Cardinal Giacomo Antonelli (1806-1876), the powerful Secretary of State of the Papal States under Pius IX. Antonelli was known for his sharp, dark Italian features - not a flattering comparison for a young woman. %%
— Elle ressemble au cardinal Antonelli, reprit Gavini.

%% 083.0134 %%
%% 2026-02-09T13:10:00 RSR: Marie's backhanded defense of Julia - "she's not old, twenty-eight or twenty-nine at most" - while slipping in that "Italian faces fade quickly." This strategic social undermining maintains an innocent protective facade while delivering devastating criticism. %%
— Oh ! elle n'est pas vieille, dis-je, vingt-huit à vingt-neuf ans au plus, peut-être moins; vous savez les figures italiennes se fanent vite.
