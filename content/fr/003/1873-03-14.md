%% %% 2026-01-07T10:30:00 RSR: ENHANCED CONTEXT - This entry documents Marie's surveillance methods and reveals the geography of desire in Nice.
THE FOUR-HORSE CARRIAGE AS SOCIAL MARKER: The Duke's "voiture a quatre chevaux" was exceptional even among the wealthy. Most visitors to Nice traveled with two-horse carriages; four horses signaled extreme wealth and status. The Duke of Hamilton, heir to one of Scotland's largest fortunes, could afford such display. For Marie, recognizing this carriage from sound alone ("j'entends un bruit de voiture") shows how attuned she was to the Duke's movements.
RUE DE FRANCE AND GIOIA'S RESIDENCE: The Rue de France ran parallel to the Promenade des Anglais, connecting key locations in Nice's fashionable quarter. Marie's ability to track the Duke's carriage "allant vers Gioia" indicates she knows exactly where his mistress lives. This surveillance was possible because Nice's elite quarter was compact and carriages were distinctive. Marie could literally watch her rival from her study window.
THE BADEN-BADEN CONNECTION: Marie's revelation about her grandfather's casual meeting with the Duke in a Baden shop is crucial context. Baden-Baden was a German spa town that served as summer counterpart to Nice's winter season - the same aristocratic circuit, different location. The family had been in the Duke's social orbit before Marie developed her obsession. Her regret at not cultivating this connection reveals sophisticated understanding of how aristocratic networks function through accumulated social capital.
THE MOTHER'S ILLNESS: The marginal note "Maman garde toujours le lit" recurs through this period. Marie's mother suffered chronic ailments, likely psychosomatic given family stresses (separation from husband, difficult lawsuit). This illness affected the family's social capacity - fewer calls paid, fewer receptions hosted - limiting Marie's exposure to the very society she craved.
MONACO AND THE TIR AUX PIGEONS: Marie's determination to attend the April pigeon shooting "si Dieu veut" sets up the central event of the spring season. The Tir aux Pigeons (established Monaco 1872) attracted aristocrats from across Europe, with prizes up to 20,000 francs. The Duke as commissioner would certainly attend. For Marie, it represented her best chance for proximity to her obsession. %% %%
