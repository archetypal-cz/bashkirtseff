%% 2026-02-09T14:05:00 RSR: Major gender manifesto in letter to Collignon. Marie's casino metaphor is brilliantly precise: men have "thirty-six chances" (the 36 numbers on a roulette wheel - multiple career paths), women have "only one, the zero like the house" (marriage as the single number that the house always plays). The house "always wins" supposedly, but "there's winning and winning." Her rejection of feminist equality as "stupid utopia" and "bad form" while declaring "I only have the skin of a woman" reveals a position that is neither feminist nor anti-feminist but profoundly personal - she doesn't want women's rights in general, she wants to be recognised as the exceptional being she feels herself to be. %%
