%% Je plie le journal et je le cache dans ma commode. Nous allons à l'église à pied (robe brune, bien). A peine retournées il arrive beaucoup de monde: les Fedoroff, les Anitchkoff, M. La cérémonie du mariage elle-même ressemble à un enterrement, pas dans ce cas-là, mais en général. Si on pouvait s'en passer, car c'est comme de l'eau froide qu'on jette sur du feu. Moi-même je l'ai éprouvé quelquefois on commençait à danser et lorsque j'allais changer de robe je ne m'amusais plus, et j'étais refroidie. Mais puisque cette pill est nécessaire. Je crois à la mort de Mme Savelieff parce que je l'ai vu morte, mais je ne puis croire à celle d'Abramovitch, il me semble qu'il n'est pas mort, qu'il doit revenir. A propos des morts, j'ai cassé ma glace, Dina la sienne, et hier le nez me démangeait. Je tâche de ne pas croire aux superstitions. Nous sommes toutes les trois, maman, ma tante et moi descendues au jardin en attendant la voiture, mais Mme Howard vient et notre voiture deux minutes après, les grands, maman, ma tante, Mme Howard et Walitsky se placent dans la nôtre et nous, moi, Hélène, Dina et Lise dans la leur. Et malheureux que nous sommes ! Nous nous promenons pendant une heure et demie rien que sur la promenade, mais heureusement nous étions occupées à parler l'une à l'autre et étions très calmement mises et ombrelles déployées, de sorte que nous n'avions pas trop l'air dimanche et promenade. I fold the newspaper and hide it in my chest of drawers. We go to church on foot (brown dress, good). Barely back, a great many people arrive: the Fedoroffs, the Anitchkoffs, M. Woerman, Baron de Bach, and a few more domestic dogs. I go out with Paul for a few minutes. I like going out as a pair, not having the carriage packed like a barrel of herrings.
%% 2026-02-19T20:38:00 OPS: NOTE: "packed like a barrel of herrings" — English idiom would be "packed like sardines" but translator's choice preserves Marie's French image "un tonneau de harengs"; acceptable (sev C) %% *Diadia* is at our house too. I go to my room and everyone goes up to the drawing room. I see this article -- how cold it seems to me. The wedding ceremony itself resembles a funeral -- not in this case, but in general. If one could do without it, for it is like cold water thrown on fire. It seems to me that immediately after the wedding, love must take another form and weaken considerably. I have often noticed that one enjoys oneself much more without preparation. I myself have experienced it -- sometimes we would begin to dance, and when I went to change my dress I no longer had fun and was cooled off. But since this ==pill== is necessary. I cannot at all imagine what he might say, what he says to her. He seems unbelievable as a husband. I believe in the death of Mme Savelieff because I saw her dead, but I cannot believe in that of Abramovitch -- it seems to me he is not dead, that he must come back. Speaking of the dead, I broke my mirror, Dina hers, and yesterday my nose itched. I try not to believe in superstitions. It seems to me that it is not Christian; I have my doubts, and I shall ask no one, not even priests, for all men are nothing but men, and each must therefore believe and understand for himself, for there is only God and oneself when it comes to religion. The three of us, Maman, my aunt, and I, went down to the garden to wait for the carriage, but Mme Howard comes and our carriage two minutes later; the elders -- Maman, my aunt, Mme Howard, and Walitsky -- take ours, and we -- myself, Helene, Dina, and Lise -- take theirs. And wretched as we are! We drive for an hour and a half along nothing but the Promenade, but fortunately we were busy talking to one another and were very quietly dressed, with parasols open, so that we did not look too much like Sunday-on-the-Promenade. As for me, I saw almost no one, for I took care to keep talking; I detest people who put themselves on display. We were passing the Villa Wittgenstein for the tenth time when I say to Dina:

I should like to sound her out one of these days; I shall try. When we arrive at their house, they show us their new dresses, which are very ordinary, not to say ugly, but I ==highly admired them,== because they themselves are enchanted with them.
