# Translation Memory (English)

Established translations for consistency across the English edition.

## People

### Maman
- Translation: Maman (keep French)
- Context: Marie's mother. Use "Maman" throughout — this is how Marie refers to her.
- Notes: Do NOT translate to "Mother" or "Mama" — the French form is part of Marie's voice

### Duke of Hamilton
- Translation: Duke of Hamilton
- Context: Object of Marie's romantic interest in Nice
- Notes: Use full title on first mention per entry, then "the Duke" or "Hamilton"

### Prince Gagarine
- Translation: Prince Gagarin
- Context: Russian aristocrat in Nice society
- Notes: Use anglicized Russian form (no final -e)

## Key Terms

### toilette
- Translation: dress / outfit / ensemble
- Context: 1870s usage = the complete outfit, NOT "toilet"
- Notes: "Change one's toilette" → "change one's dress"

### en amazone
- Translation: in a riding habit / sidesaddle
- Context: Women's riding costume with long skirt for sidesaddle
- Notes: NOT "like an Amazon"

### canaille
- Translation: scoundrel / rascal / rogue
- Context: Pejorative for lower classes; Marie uses it dramatically
- Notes: Context-dependent — sometimes keep French in italics

### promenade
- Translation: promenade / walk
- Context: Both the place and the social activity of walking
- Notes: Keep "promenade" when referring to the specific location

## Register & Crude Language

### crever (vulgar for "die")
- Translation: croak / kick the bucket
- Context: Deliberately crude — preserve the register
- Notes: Do NOT soften to "pass away" or "die"
