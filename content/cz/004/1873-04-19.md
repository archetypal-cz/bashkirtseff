THE PRIEST'S REBUKE: Marie's confession yielded a direct accusation - "vous aimez a vous faire remarquer, vous etes vaine" (you like to draw attention to yourself, you are vain). This was a standard confession response to young aristocratic women obsessed with appearance and social visibility. Notably, Marie records the rebuke without apparent contrition, proceeding immediately to describe her Easter outfit in detail. THE EASTER OUTFIT AND GALVE IMITATION: Marie wears "la robe blanche neuve" with "coiffure par Allard a la comtesse de Galve" - a new white dress with hair styled in imitation of Countess de Galve. This explicit copying reflects Marie's strategy of modeling herself on the Russian aristocrat who moves in the Duke's circle. The professional hairdresser Allard served Nice's elite. DARKENED EYEBROWS - PREMATURE COSMETICS: Marie's marginal note - "J'avais les sourcils noircis et on le voyait. C'etait affreux" - reveals her use of cosmetics inappropriate for her age (13-14). Darkening eyebrows was acceptable for married women but improper for unmarried girls. That "on le voyait" (it was visible) suggests crude application. The later Marie is embarrassed by this premature attempt at sophistication. LORD CARLOS HAMILTON - THE BROTHER: Carlos Hamilton was the Duke's younger brother. Marie's encounter with him in town - and Walitsky's teasing "Eh bien! pourquoi vous ne sautez pas de la voiture?" - indicates others in the household were aware of her Hamilton obsession. Her blushing and stammering ("j'ai balbutie quelque chose") exposed her feelings. Dina's observation "Elle en rougit meme" confirms family awareness. MLLE DE GALVE AT ORTHODOX CHURCH: Marie's detailed observation of Galve - "horriblement mal coiffee," "ne savait ou se mettre," "pas sure d'elle" - shows satisfaction at her rival's discomfort. Galve, likely Catholic, attended the Orthodox service ("une eglise etrangere") with Anglican companions, appearing awkward and ill-dressed. Marie's schadenfreude is palpable. VISUALIZING THE DUKE IN CHURCH: Marie notes she can only "me representer sa figure" in church. This suggests religious settings triggered her romantic imagination, perhaps because of the contemplative atmosphere or because church was where she first saw him. The Russian phrase "mon coeur se serre" (my heart contracts) indicates Marie found her native language more expressive for certain emotions. RUSSIA TRIP CANCELED: "Je crois que nous n'allons pas en Russie" - the family lawsuit that threatened to take them east for the summer may be resolving. For Marie, remaining in Western Europe means "plus de chance de le rencontrer" - the Baden-Vienna-Nice circuit where she might encounter the Duke. %%
