# January 1873 - Summary

## Main Topics
- Marie's growing fascination with the Duke of Hamilton
- Social events in Nice during the winter season
- Horse racing and yacht watching
- Marie's self-reflection and emotional development

## Key Events
- January 11-15: Early entries establishing Marie's daily life and social circle
- January 18-20: Increasing attention to the Duke's movements and activities
- January 24: Observations of social dynamics at various events
- January 26: Sighting of the Duke's yacht and his absence at the races
- January 29: A day of personal activities (horseback riding, music, English lessons)

## People
- Duke of Hamilton: The primary object of Marie's romantic interest, a race commissioner and prominent figure in Nice society
- The Howards: Part of the international social circle in Nice
- Prince Gagarine: Another notable figure in Nice's high society
- Wittgenstein: Member of a prominent European aristocratic family, associated with the Duke
- Boreel: Another potential suitor, often compared unfavorably to the Duke
- Gioia: The Duke's companion, a source of jealousy for Marie

- Horse racing as a major social event
