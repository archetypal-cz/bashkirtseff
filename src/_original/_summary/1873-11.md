# November 1873: The Approaching Catastrophe

## Overview

November 1873 represents the penultimate month before Marie learns of Hamilton's marriage, and her diary entries reflect an increasingly frantic state of mind. The month is dominated by Marie's desperate attempts to see Hamilton, her growing awareness that something significant is happening in his life, and her physical and emotional deterioration. The entries reveal a young woman on the edge of a breakdown, clinging to increasingly unlikely hopes while simultaneously preparing for devastation.

## Main Topics and Themes

### The Final Pursuit
- **Desperate Positioning**: Marie's increasingly bold attempts to place herself where Hamilton might see her
- **The Promenade Vigils**: Daily walks timed to coincide with Hamilton's potential appearances
- **Church Encounters**: Strategic attendance at churches where Hamilton might appear
- **Physical Pursuit**: Following his carriage, monitoring his movements through Nice

### Signs of Hamilton's Impending Marriage
- **Absence Patterns**: Hamilton's decreasing presence in Nice
- **Behavioral Changes**: When seen, he appears different - more serious, less carefree
- **Social Whispers**: Increasing hints from society members about his changed circumstances
- **Furniture and Preparations**: Continued references to purchases suggesting establishment of a household

### Physical and Mental Deterioration
- **Health Decline**: Severe weight loss, inability to eat, constant trembling
- **Sleep Disturbances**: Nightmares about Hamilton, inability to sleep
- **Emotional Volatility**: Rapid swings between hope and despair
- **Family Concerns**: Increasing worry from family members about Marie's state

### Social Consequences
- **Complete Isolation**: The Tutcheff conflict has now fully excluded the family from Nice society
- **Desperate Gestures**: Marie considers increasingly inappropriate ways to attract Hamilton's attention
- **Lost Opportunities**: Awareness that her social isolation prevents any natural meeting with Hamilton
- **Reputation Concerns**: Growing awareness that her obvious infatuation is becoming publicly known

## Key Events

1. **November 2**: All Saints' Day - Marie's desperate hope to see Hamilton at church
2. **November 5**: Spotting Hamilton's empty carriage, leading to frantic speculation
3. **November 10**: Learning Hamilton has been seen with "a lady" - devastating news
4. **November 14**: Marie's confession to her diary that she would "die for one word from him"
5. **November 18**: Complete breakdown after seeing Hamilton pass without acknowledging her
6. **November 23**: Princess Ouroussoff's hints about "changes in Hamilton's life"
7. **November 26**: Marie's mother threatens to send her to Russia if she doesn't recover
8. **November 30**: End of month reflection - Marie admits she has "lived only for him" all month

## People Mentioned

- **Duke of Hamilton**: Increasingly absent, when seen appears changed and serious
- **"The Lady"**: Mysterious woman reportedly seen with Hamilton
- **Marie's Mother**: Increasingly concerned and threatening intervention
- **Princess Ouroussoff**: Source of painful hints about Hamilton's situation
- **Gioia**: Also suffering, apparently abandoned by Hamilton as well
- **Dr. Friedlander**: Consulting about Marie's declining health
- **Paul**: Continues to report Hamilton sightings to Marie
- **Various Nice Society Members**: Who now openly pity or mock Marie

## Marie's Mental State

November shows Marie in a state of:
- **Obsessive Fixation**: Every thought and action revolves around Hamilton
- **Delusional Hope**: Interpreting neutral events as meaningful signs
- **Physical Manifestation**: Her emotional state causing severe physical symptoms
- **Suicidal Ideation**: References to preferring death to life without Hamilton

## Social and Cultural Context

- **Victorian Propriety**: The constraints preventing Marie from directly approaching Hamilton
- **Marriage Season**: November as a time when engagements were often announced
- **Social Telegraph**: How news of Hamilton's situation spreads through whispers and hints
- **Family Honor**: The increasing concern about Marie's obvious infatuation damaging family reputation

## Setting Details

- **Nice Promenade**: The main stage for Marie's desperate attempts to encounter Hamilton
- **Churches**: Various churches where Marie hopes for meetings
- **Monte Carlo**: Where Hamilton is occasionally reported but Marie cannot follow
- **The Bashkirtseff Villa**: Increasingly a prison where Marie watches from windows

## Physical Symptoms Documented

- Severe weight loss ("maigre comme un squelette")
- Inability to eat ("la nourriture me dégoûte")
- Trembling ("mes mains tremblent constamment")
- Cardiac symptoms ("mon cœur bat si fort")
- Insomnia and nightmares

## Foreshadowing the Crisis

The November entries build inexorably toward the December revelation. Key warning signs include:
- Hamilton's increasing absence from Nice
- His changed demeanor when seen
- Society's knowing looks and hints
- Gioia's similar suffering suggesting Hamilton has moved on
- References to "changes" and "news" that people won't directly share

## Family Dynamics

- **Mother's Intervention**: Threats to remove Marie from Nice
- **Family Shame**: Growing embarrassment about Marie's public displays
- **Paul's Role**: Torn between helping his sister and recognizing the futility
- **Aunt's Concerns**: Trying to interest Marie in other activities, failing completely
