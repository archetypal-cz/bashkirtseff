Je me réveille à onze heures et commence à compter mon argent. Je vois que deux mille cinq cents francs ne sont rien quand il faut les dépenser. Je ne puis m'arranger au-dessous de trois mille francs, et encore, c'est bien modeste. J'ai acheté du papier avec mes initiales, fait recouvrir l'ombrelle, commandé une paire de bottines grises (j'en ai cinq paires) des gants, des robes; il me faut des chapeaux, maintenant. [//]: # ( 2023-04-05T13:56:00 RSR: Charles Frederick Worth (1825-1895) was a British-born fashion designer who founded the House of Worth in Paris in 1858. By 1873, his fashion house at 7 Rue de la Paix was firmly established as the premier haute couture establishment in Paris, catering to royalty, aristocrats, and wealthy clients from Europe and America. Worth is often called the "father of haute couture" and revolutionized fashion with his innovative designs, use of live models, and branding techniques. The 700 francs Marie mentions for a dress (approximately $3,500-4,000 in today's currency) was indeed a substantial sum, highlighting the luxury status of Worth creations. )

[//]: # ( 2023-04-05T13:56:00 RSR: The Opéra-Comique is a famous opera house in Paris, founded in 1714. In 1873, it was located at its second Salle Favart building on Place Boieldieu. The mention of a "restaurant russe" (Russian restaurant) across from it indicates the growing Russian presence and influence in Paris during this period. )

[//]: # ( 2023-04-05T13:56:00 RSR: Porchefontaine is a district in Versailles that had a racecourse from 1864 to 1870. Marie's mention of "courses à Porchefontaine" (races at Porchefontaine) in 1873 is interesting, as historical records suggest the racecourse was no longer active by this time. It's possible that either some racing activities continued there informally, Marie was referring to a different type of racing event, or she was mistaken about the location. Horse racing was a popular social activity among the upper classes in 19th century Paris. )
