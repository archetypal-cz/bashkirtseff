# Monaco Pigeon Shoot

**Research Status**: Basic
**Last Updated**: 2025-07-05
**Diary Coverage**: Up to 1874-01-14

## Overview
Elite sporting event held at Monte Carlo in the 1870s, attracting international aristocracy and wealthy sportsmen.

## Diary References

### 1874
- **January 14**: Marie attends the first major competition ("premier grand concours") of the season. Special trains run from Nice. The event combines sport with high society gathering. Shooting takes place at the "Tir" (shooting ground) near the casino.

## Details
- Required special tickets available at principal hotels or station
- Special trains from Nice for the events
- Attracted mix of aristocrats, wealthy merchants, and international elite
- Combined competitive shooting with social networking
- Ladies attended as spectators
- Winners:
  - 1st: "M. Pooool" (Marie's nickname based on how he says "pull")
  - 2nd: Baron de Saint-Clair
  - 3rd: Prince de Chimay
  - Joe Norris disqualified after missing twice

## Social Context
- Major society event on the Riviera calendar
- Opportunity for marriage market activities
- Display of wealth and status
- Marie critical of many participants as nouveau riche ("perruquiers travestis")
