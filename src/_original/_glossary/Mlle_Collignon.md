# Mlle Collignon

**Research Status**: Basic
**Last Updated**: 2025-06-30
**Diary Coverage**: Up to 1873-05

## Overview

Mademoiselle Collignon is Marie's French governess during the Nice period (1873). She appears frequently in the diary entries, accompanying Marie on walks, visits, and various outings. Her role seems to be a combination of chaperone, companion, and educator.

## Key Characteristics

From Marie's diary entries, Mlle Collignon appears to be:
- A constant presence in Marie's daily life
- Often described as having opinions about Marie's behavior and interests
- Accompanies Marie on walks to the port, visits to friends, and shopping
- Sometimes characterized as being overwhelmed or "losing her voice" during dramatic moments
- Appears to have some conflict with Marie's romantic interests and social ambitions

## Notable Appearances

### January 1873
- Regularly accompanies Marie on visits to various families
- Present during Marie's early encounters with Boreel

### February 1873
- Continues as Marie's companion during social calls
- Witnesses Marie's growing obsession with the Duke of Hamilton

### March-April 1873
- Remains a steady presence despite Marie's emotional turmoil
- Often mentioned in context of daily routines and outings

### May 1873
- On May 3rd, accompanies Marie to the port where they spot what might be the Duke's yacht
- Described as having "lost her voice" and seeming "broken" and "more gentle and confused"

## Role in Marie's Life

Mlle Collignon represents the conventional restrictions placed on young women of Marie's class. As a governess, she serves as both educator and guardian of propriety, though Marie often seems to view her as an obstacle to her romantic pursuits and social ambitions.
