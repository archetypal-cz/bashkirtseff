# Rumpelmayer

**Research Status**: Basic
**Last Updated**: 2025-06-28
**Diary Coverage**: Referenced in 1873 entries

## Identity and Background

Rumpelmayer was a famous confectionery and pastry shop in Nice during the 19th century, frequently mentioned in Marie Bashkirtseff's diary. The establishment was part of the Rumpelmayer chain, which had several locations in fashionable European resort towns.

## Historical Context

The Rumpelmayer confectioneries were founded by Antoine Rumpelmayer, an Austrian confectioner who established luxury pastry shops in several European cities during the 19th century. The Nice location became a popular gathering place for the international elite during the winter season.

### Known Locations

- **Nice**: Located on the Promenade des Anglais, it was a fashionable meeting place for society
- **Paris**: The Rue de Rivoli location was equally prestigious
- **Monte Carlo**: Another location served the casino crowd
- **Baden-Baden**: Catered to spa visitors

## Social Significance

In Marie's diary, Rumpelmayer appears as:

1. **Social Hub**: A place where the fashionable crowd would gather for afternoon tea and pastries
2. **Observation Point**: Marie could observe society figures while appearing to be simply enjoying refreshments
3. **Status Symbol**: Being seen at Rumpelmayer indicated one's participation in high society

## Specialties

Rumpelmayer was renowned for:

- Elaborate pastries and cakes
- Hot chocolate
- Ice creams and sorbets
- Confectionery and chocolates
- Afternoon tea service
