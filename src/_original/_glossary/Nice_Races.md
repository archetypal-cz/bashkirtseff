# Nice Races (Hippodrome de Nice)

**Research Status**: Basic
**Last Updated**: 2025-07-05
**Diary Coverage**: Up to 1874-01-15

## Overview
Horse racing venue and major social event in Nice during the winter season.

## Diary References

### 1874
- **January 15**: Marie attends but feels "la plus malheureuse" (most miserable). She describes the races as where "society and toilettes were extraordinarily brilliant." High society attendees included princesses, duchesses, and viscountesses. Marie's family remains isolated while others socialize freely.

## Social Function
- Primary venue for society to see and be seen
- Display of fashion and wealth ("belles toilettes")
- Networking and matchmaking opportunities
- "On se rencontre, on se conna√Æt" (one meets, one knows each other)
- Strict social hierarchies visible in interactions

## Historical Context
- Horse racing was introduced to Nice in the 1860s
- The hippodrome was located in the Var plain
- Races ran during the winter season (December-April)
- Attracted international aristocracy and wealthy bourgeoisie
- Combined British sporting traditions with French elegance

## Marie's Experience
- Excluded from social interactions despite being well-dressed
- Witnesses others forming connections while isolated
- Represents the family's liminal social position
- Source of intense emotional pain and envy
