# Hôtel Chauvain

**Research Status**: Basic
**Last Updated**: 2025-07-05
**Diary Coverage**: Up to 1874-03-01

## Overview
Hotel in Nice that served as an art exhibition venue in the 1870s. On March 1, 1874, it hosted an exhibition of marine paintings by the renowned artist Aivazovsky.

## Historical Context
During the Belle Époque, hotels in Nice often functioned as cultural centers, hosting art exhibitions, concerts, and social gatherings for the international elite wintering on the Riviera. The Hôtel Chauvain appears to have been one such establishment.

## Cultural Significance
The hotel's hosting of Aivazovsky's exhibition indicates it was a prestigious venue capable of displaying works by internationally recognized artists. Such exhibitions were important social events for Nice's cosmopolitan community.
