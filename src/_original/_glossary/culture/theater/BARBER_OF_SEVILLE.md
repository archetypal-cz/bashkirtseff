# The Barber of Seville (Le Barbier de Séville)

**Research Status**: Moderate
**Last Updated**: 2025-07-06
**Diary Coverage**: Up to 1874-04-26

## Identity
Opera buffa by Gioachino Rossini (1816), based on Beaumarchais' comedy. One of the most popular comic operas of the 19th century.

## Diary References

### 1874
- **April 26**: Attends evening performance: "Le soir je vais au théâtre. 'Le Barbier de Séville' (robe de promenade, pas mal)." Notes her outfit choice.

## Plot Summary
- Count Almaviva courts Rosina
- Helped by barber Figaro
- Outwits guardian Dr. Bartolo
- Famous arias: "Largo al factotum," "Una voce poco fa"
- Comic disguises and tricks

## Context
- Performed at Nice theaters regularly
- Light entertainment for society
- Fashion display opportunity
- Social event as much as cultural

## Cultural Significance
- Rossini's masterpiece
- Influenced comic opera tradition
- Beaumarchais trilogy adaptation
- Universal themes of young love vs. old guardians
