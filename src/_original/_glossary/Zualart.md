# Zualart

**Research Status**: Basic
**Last Updated**: 2025-07-06
**Diary Coverage**: Up to 1874-03-28

## Identity
Man in Lambertye's social circle whom Marie finds particularly repulsive.

## Diary References

### 1874
- **March 28**: Marie describes him as "une horreur sans nom" (a nameless horror) who stares at her alongside Lambertye, Furstenberg, and Galve on the train to Monaco. She writes: "Ce Zualart, j'aurais voulu arracher les yeux ce matin!" (This Zualart, I would have wanted to tear out his eyes this morning!).

## Context
- Part of Lambertye's male social group
- Participates in staring at Marie in insulting manner
- Name suggests possible Swiss or German origin
- Represents the disrespectful behavior Marie endures
- One of the men Lambertye "shows" Marie to
- Provokes violent fantasies of revenge in Marie
