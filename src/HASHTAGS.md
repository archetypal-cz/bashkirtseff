# Hashtags

What follows is a list of french-named hashtags for events, people, places, works etc. with a short English explanation of what it is on the next line (indented to match the list item). When we're looking around for what to do and encounter an obvious hashtag without explanation, let's provide one or expand on it. 

We should also write down comma-separated ISO dates of diary entries where this topic gets mentioned - as the next line of this list item.

Hashtags must ONLY contain western ASCII characters, and are not to be case sensitive.

Translations will the be able to take this file and use it to create an index/dictionary/cross-reference material.

## The list

- #Alexander_II
  Emperor of Russia (1855-1881), known as the "Tsar Liberator" for his emancipation of the serfs. His encounter with Marie's mother shows the family's high social standing.
- #Emile_Zola 
  French novelist, journalist, and playwright (1840-1902), a major figure in the literary school of naturalism and an important contributor to the development of theatrical naturalism.
- #Edmond_de_Goncourt
  French writer and publisher (1822-1896) who, with his brother Jules, was a founder of the Académie Goncourt and known for detailed descriptions of everyday life and realism.
- #Guy_de_Maupassant
  French writer (1850-1893) considered one of the fathers of the modern short story and known for his direct and efficient style.
- #Document_Humain
  "Human document" - a term used by naturalist writers in the 19th century, especially Émile Zola, for authentic records of human experiences and behavior that served as material for realistic literature.
- #Poltava
  City in eastern Ukraine where Marie Bashkirtseff was born, part of the Russian Empire during her lifetime.
- #Maman
  Mother of Marie Bashkirtseff, frequently mentioned throughout her diary.
- #Dina
  Cousin of Marie Bashkirtseff, daughter of her uncle Georges, who appears as a companion in her diary.
- #Walitzky
  Lucien Walitsky, a Polish doctor who became a close friend of the Bashkirtseff family.
- #Paul_Grigorievitch_Bashkirtseff
  Grandfather of Marie Bashkirtseff, a general in the Russian army who was promoted after the Crimean War.
- #Crimean_War
  Military conflict fought from 1853 to 1856 between the Russian Empire and an alliance of the Ottoman Empire, France, Britain, and Sardinia.
- #Babanine
  Marie Bashkirtseff's mother's family name, described as old provincial nobility with claimed Tatar origins.
- #Lermontov
  Mikhail Lermontov (1814-1841), a Russian Romantic writer, poet and painter, sometimes called "the poet of the Caucasus".
- #Pushkin
  Alexander Pushkin (1799-1837), Russian poet, playwright, and novelist considered by many to be the greatest Russian poet and the founder of modern Russian literature.
- #Lord_Byron
  George Gordon Byron (1788-1824), English poet and a leading figure in the Romantic movement, known for his aristocratic excesses, enormous debts, numerous love affairs, and self-imposed exile.
- #Caucasus
  Mountainous region between the Black Sea and the Caspian Sea, which was a significant frontier in the Russian Empire and a place of exile for many Russian officers and poets.
- #Julie_Cornelius
  Grandmother of Marie Bashkirtseff, who married at fifteen years old and had nine children.
- #Georges
  Uncle of Marie Bashkirtseff, described as a troubled and destructive figure in the family who caused many problems with his behavior.
- #Viatka
  City in Russia (now Kirov) where Marie's uncle Georges was sent into exile.
- #St_Petersburg
  Capital of the Russian Empire at the time, where Marie's mother traveled with Georges.
- #Crimea
  Peninsula in the Black Sea, part of the Russian Empire during Marie's lifetime, known for its seaside resorts popular with Russian aristocracy.
- #Paul
  Brother of Marie Bashkirtseff, mentioned in relation to their father's attempt to gain custody.
- #Gavronzi
  Estate of the Bashkirtseff family near Poltava, where a dramatic family scene took place.
- #Domenica
  Wife of Georges Bashkirtseff, who obtained permission for his transfer from Viatka to Akhtyrka.
- #Akhtyrka
  City in Ukraine's Kharkiv region, known for its Orthodox monastery and miraculous icon of the Mother of God, where Georges was transferred during his exile.
- #Madame_Brenne
  French governess of Marie Bashkirtseff, who taught her drawing and died of tuberculosis in Crimea in 1868.
- #Romanoff
  A wealthy bachelor who became involved with the Bashkirtseff family, courted by Marie's mother.
- #Vassilissa_Babanine
  Aunt of Marie Bashkirtseff, sister of her grandfather, described as a once beautiful and elegant woman who died in poverty.
- #Nadine_Martinoff
  An orphan adopted by Vassilissa Babanine, who later married Alexandre Babanine at age fifteen.
- #Tcherniakovka
  Estate of the Babanine family, where Nadine Martinoff went to live after leaving her adoptive mother.
- #Alexandre
  Uncle of Marie Bashkirtseff who married Nadine Martinoff, described as a practical and calculating person.
- #Kharkoff
  Major city in eastern Ukraine (now Kharkiv) where the Bashkirtseff family spent time during the Romanoff courtship.
- #Yalta
  Seaside resort town in Crimea, popular with Russian aristocracy in the 19th century.
- #Odessa
  Major port city on the Black Sea, where the Bashkirtseff family stayed with Romanoff.
- #Vienna
  Capital of the Austrian Empire, where the family spent a month enjoying high society life after Romanoff's marriage.
- #Baden_Baden
  German spa town popular with European aristocracy in the 19th century, where the family stayed in 1870.
- #Lucien_Walitsky
  Polish doctor and close friend of the Bashkirtseff family, particularly devoted to Marie's mother. He served as the family physician but was poorly compensated.
- #Berthe_Boyd
  English girl who Marie met in Baden-Baden, daughter of a prominent family and part of the elite children's social circle.
- #Remy_de_Gonzales_Moreno
  Thirteen-year-old son of the Gonzalès family who was Berthe Boyd's "sweetheart" and showed interest in Marie.
- #Gonzales_Family
  Argentine family the Bashkirtseffs met in Baden-Baden, consisting of Mr. Gonzalès (who was attracted to Marie's mother), his jealous wife, and their son Rémy.
- #Geneva
  Swiss city where the Bashkirtseff family took refuge during the Franco-Prussian War of 1870.
- #Russia
  The Russian Empire, where Marie Bashkirtseff was born and spent her early years. She often comments on Russian society and character.
- #Madame_Melnikoff
  Russian governess who taught Marie music and dance, described as an educated and romantic woman who left her position dramatically.
- #Petipa
  Famous French ballet dancer and choreographer Marius Petipa (1818-1910), who worked in Russia. Young Marie imagined herself as this celebrated artist.
- #Lola
  Daughter of Georges and his wife, half-sister to Dina.
- #Etienne
  Son of Georges and his wife, who died at age sixteen.
- #Sophie_Dolgikoff
  Sixteen-year-old Russian governess who succeeded Madame Melnikoff. Her romantic troubles are described in the diary.
- #Mont_Blanc
  Highest mountain in the Alps and Western Europe, visible from Geneva on clear days. Marie drew it from her window at the Huberkoller pension.
- #Bachmakoff
  Former vice-governor of Poltava who met Marie's mother in Geneva and remembered her from her youth.
- #Chtcherbine
  Young boy who was placed in Dr. Hactius's boarding school in Geneva by Mr. Bachmakoff.
- #Hactius
  Doctor who ran a famous boarding school in Geneva where young Chtcherbine was placed.
